To understand the history of the Great House of Bayle and thus the origins of their Kingdom in the West one must approach the subject from the context of the Combine Empire. The House of Bayle had solidified itself as one of the twelve Great Houses of Men near the end of the Combine era. Hectimus Bayle had been granted lands by the Combine Emperor along the Coldwind coast several hundred leagues to the north of the capital city for his heroics in the Battle of the White Lily. It was here that he amassed a fortune, harvesting an exotic purple dye from the glands of the Coldwind octopi. In time a city-state named Qeynos was formed and from then on it was the seat of the great Bayle family. To this day, while the House of Bayle has found mercantile opportunities in many different industries over the years, dye, specifically purple dye, still remains as their number one export and the main source of their incredible wealth.

Though scholars agree there were many events that led to the fall of the Combine Empire, perhaps the most apparent was the War of Plagues which saw the capital city under constant siege both from without and within. During this time Valeria Bayle, the daughter of Antonious Bayle was to be wed to the soon to be crown prince, Cahirim VI but both were stricken with the plague before their wedding day. With the city slipping into chaos with each passing day the Bayle’s fled to their northern stronghold in the city of Qeynos.

With the empire crumbling under the encroaching pressure from all sides and with a clear view of the approaching chaos that would ensure, Antonious chose to wed his other daughter Tatiana Bayle to High Lord Dara of the Erudian House Tal-Azroth. House Tal’Azroth had been at the forefront of Combine philosophy and education for the last 100 years and had a history that seated them deep in Combine legacy. The marriage was a political move on the part of Antonious to solidify his ties with the Combine Empire so that, upon it’s inevitable fall which he foresaw, he would have a strong Combine influence to garner support from the masses.

Combining their incredible wealth and power, the two great houses struck a deal with House Ironforge and Clothspinner in the form of several hundred acres of land, some of which Lord Antonious had no right to divy up at the time, and an ample amount of gold. Antonious now had a source of ongoing economic support but he required an army as no man is able to hold onto his power without a sizeable number of steel and iron in his hands. He found one in the northern houses of Redjford and Thenrick. While difficult to sway as they had never been fond of the Combine rulers to the south, Antonious was eventually able to bring them to reason though not without a significant cost. Lord Antonious sent two of his sons to be wards for House Redjford, while Lord Dana of house Tal’Azroth was forced to wed his only daughter, a mere girl of 15 years, to the 84 year old Lord Perun of House Thenrick.

Now with an army, wealth, and political backing, Lord Antonious seemed fit to make his move yet he still desired one thing more, religion. While believing in no gods himself, Antonious understood the power of theology and its sway over the people. The Combine had long neglected religion as a tool of control and Antonious thought to rectify that. The newly formed Church of Thunder was led by a zealous priest by the name of Rylin Moltor. It served as a connection between the common farmers and peasants of the Karanas and the Knight of Truth to the east. Antonious made Rylin a general and promised him lands as well as priestly authority in his new order.

After the capital city fell and the Combine Emperor fled to the Hijazrian fortress of Bastion in the Rathe mountains, Antonious turned his armies south to quell Lord Grimrot and his army of Plaguebringers. Years of preparation had left Antonious with a powerful, near unstoppable army and Rylin Moltor proved to be just as capable and zealous a general as he was a priest. Antonious’ army cut through the forces of Lord Grimrot like butter and within just a few, short months the conflict was over. Lord Grimrot, however, and many of his followers fled into the Serpent Spine and, though Antonious sent countless battalions into the mountains to root them out, they have not been seen since.

Though some theorize that the Combine Empire would not have fallen if Lord Antonious Bayle had arrived earlier, nonetheless, the people of the empire greeted him with open arms. The ruling class of the Combine Empire retreated to the Rathe mountains broken economically and militarily. Today it resides as little more than a hollow shell of its former glory. Lord Antonious Bayle seized the lands from Everfrost Peaks to the southernmost regions of the Karanas. His great gamble had been a success. He was crowned king and granted many lands to houses Ironforge and Clothspinner and established the Church of Thunder headed by Rylin Moltor. To this day his family continues to rule from the seat of Qeynos.

Today, the Kingdom of Bayle is ruled by Antonious IV. It has grown into a powerful nation with close ties to the Eastern Commonwealth of Freeport as well as the Halasian Federation to the north. The Church of Thunder has been abandoned by the royal family for the Church of Rodcet Nife, the Prime Healer after years of religious stagnation and political embarrassment. Rumors abound as to the return of Lord Grimrot and his followers the Bloodsabers though these rumors cannot be corroborated. Some, however, even claim the king’s brother, Kyle Bayle to be involved somehow. The royal family has yet to comment on any of this speculation.
